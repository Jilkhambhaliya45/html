MASALA DOSA

RECIPE
Ingredients:

For the Dosa Batter: 1 cup uncooked rice 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram) 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds) Water (for soaking and grinding) Salt to taste For the Potato Filling (Masala): 3-4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed 1 onion, finely chopped 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste) 1 teaspoon mustard seeds 1 teaspoon cumin seeds A pinch of asafoetida (hing) 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder A few curry leaves 1 tablespoon oil Salt to taste Chopped coriander leaves for garnishing

For the Dosa:

Dosa batter Oil or ghee (clarified butter) for cooking Water (for thinning the batter, if needed)

Instructions: For the Dosa Batter:

Wash the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds together in plenty of water. Soak them in water for about 4-6 hours. Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them to a smooth batter using water as needed. The batter should be slightly coarse in texture. Add salt to the batter and mix well. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. For the Potato Filling (Masala): Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Sauté for a moment. Add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions turn translucent. Stir in the turmeric powder and mashed potatoes. Mix well. Cook the mixture for a few minutes, allowing the flavors to blend. Add salt to taste. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and set aside. For Making the Dosa: Heat a non-stick dosa tawa (griddle) and lightly grease it with oil or ghee. Take a ladleful of dosa batter and pour it onto the center of the tawa. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin, even layer. Drizzle a little oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa. Cook the dosa on medium heat until it turns golden brown and crispy. Place a portion of the potato masala filling in the center of the dosa. Gently fold the dosa in half or roll it into a cylindrical shape. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar. Enjoy your delicious homemade Masala Dosa! Note: The dosa batter should be of pouring consistency. If it's too thick, you can add a little water to thin it out.
